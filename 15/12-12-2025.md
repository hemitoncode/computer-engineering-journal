### Learning/Reflections:
I was unaware what the circuit was due to lack of context, so I asked Kush to explain everything. Once I understood the requirements for this project, I made a schematic using https://www.circuit-diagram.org/editor/.

I soldified my understanding of transistors, spefically NPN and the 3 pins they have (collector, base, emitter). The collector is where current enters the transistor, so it is important to use a resistor for this part if your non-restricted can get high (you do not want to blow out the transistor!). The base is the **control pin.** It allows the transistor to act as a switch by blocking the flow of current coming from collector when no current comes through it. Therefore, whatever is connected to the base must have a state in which current is cut. This can be done by adding a switch which completes flow of current by inserting a metal wire when pressed. **AND** finally the emitter is where the electrons leave the transistor. It is usually connected to the GND.
